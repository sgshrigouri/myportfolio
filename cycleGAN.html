Cycle GAN for MNIST

This project implements a Cycle GAN for image-to-image translation on the MNIST dataset, transforming handwritten digits between domains (e.g., digits 0-4 to digits 5-9). Cycle GANs use two generators and two discriminators with cycle-consistency loss to learn unpaired image translations, enabling the model to convert images from one domain to another without paired training data.

The architecture consists of two generators (G_XY and G_YX) and two discriminators (D_X and D_Y). The generators use residual blocks for feature transformation, while the discriminators classify images as real or fake using a PatchGAN approach. The training process involves adversarial loss, cycle-consistency loss, and identity loss to ensure realistic translations and preserve content.
